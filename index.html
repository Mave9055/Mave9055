Non-Clinical Psychoeducational Curriculum

Structured trauma education for recovery, veterans, reentry, and peer-support programs.

Capitol Contracts LLC develops the What Really Happened (WRH) project system: a structured, non-clinical curriculum that teaches survival patterns, addiction cycles, freeze responses, shame loops, and nervous-system adaptation as predictable mechanisms — not character failures.

The system connects a lived-experience memoir, public articles, a 5-week introduction, a 30-session pilot package, and a full 75-session master curriculum for institutional review and implementation planning.

Who This Is For

WRH is designed for organizations that need clear trauma education without requiring therapy participation, clinical disclosure, or diagnostic framing.

Recovery and Reentry Programs

Plain-language education for shame, avoidance, relapse patterns, emotional shutdown, accountability, and survival behavior.

Veteran-Support Organizations

Mechanism-first language that can reach guarded audiences who may resist therapy-style framing.

Peer and Community Programs

Facilitator-ready education tools for structured discussion, pattern recognition, and non-clinical support.

How the System Fits Together

The public work is organized as a clear pipeline from source story to implementation-ready curriculum.

1. MemoirFrom the Storm to the Fire provides the lived story and source material.
2. ArticlesPublic counter-narratives translate survival patterns into plain language.
3. CoreTop 10 Core introduces the WRH language in a shorter 5-week track.
4. PilotThe 30-session package supports field testing and implementation planning.
5. MasterThe 75-session curriculum provides the full structured WRH architecture.
